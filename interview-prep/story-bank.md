# Interview Story Bank — Varun

## STAR+R Stories

### Story 1: Diagnosing Electrochromic Material Failure (Reliability)
**S:** During PhD research on electrochromic materials, noticed that device performance degraded unpredictably over cycling.
**T:** Needed to identify root cause and establish predictive failure criteria.
**A:** Systematically characterized degradation using UV-Vis-NIR, cyclic voltammetry, and spectroelectrochemistry. Identified three distinct failure modes: oxidative degradation of the chromophore, interface delamination at the electrode, and redox instability under cycling. Developed accelerated aging tests based on physics-of-failure methodology.
**R:** Established clear structure-stability relationships for 15+ material variants. Provided design rules that guided subsequent molecular design toward more stable systems.
**+R:** Learned that systematic failure mode analysis is more valuable than trial-and-error optimization. This experience directly translates to industrial reliability engineering.

### Story 3: AFOSR Defense Coatings Process (Cross-functional)

**T:** Develop a reliable deposition process that met optical specifications.
**A:** Co-led the effort with synthetic chemists who made the molecules and materials engineers who tested the optical properties. I developed the surface preparation protocols and CVD deposition strategies. Used DOE to optimize process parameters.
**R:** Achieved reproducible coatings meeting defense specifications. The process was adopted by the broader team.
**+R:** Working at the intersection of synthesis and engineering taught me to speak both languages, which is critical in any industrial R&D environment.

### Story 4: Bio-based Polymer Scale-up (Process Development)

**T:** Develop reproducible process specifications that would work at scale.

**+R:** This experience reinforced that process development is as much about documentation and statistical rigor as it is about chemistry.
